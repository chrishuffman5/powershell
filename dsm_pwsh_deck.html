CHAPTER I ANXIETY SPECTRUM
— observation chart 01 —
F41.0
F41.0 · Diagnosis 01

Cursora­phobia

Phobic · Acute Onset
CLINICAL TRANSCRIPT — INTAKE
PS> _▮
# patient froze for 47 seconds
PS> [EXIT CODE: 1 — fled to Notepad]
§ Clinical Description

Pathological fear of the blinking cursor. Patients report a creeping dread when faced with an empty prompt, often described as "the void staring back."

Acute episodes involve frantic mouse movement toward any visible icon. The patient may verbalize phrases such as "where do I even type" or "is it broken?"

Comorbidity: Frequently presents alongside Right-Click Dependency Syndrome and a deep mistrust of monospaced fonts.

CHAPTER II COMPULSIVE DISORDERS
— observation chart 02 —
F42.1
F42.1 · Diagnosis 02

Mousoleum Syndrome

Compulsive · Progressive
OBSERVED BEHAVIOR — TIME-MOTION STUDY
# task: move 12 files
PS> Move-Item *.log ./archive
Estimated duration: 0.2s

# patient's chosen method:
Drag & drop x 12
Actual duration: 4m 18s
§ Clinical Description

The compulsive entombment of all productivity within mouse-driven workflows. Sufferers will click through seventeen nested menus rather than type four characters.

In severe cases, patients have been observed dragging files across three monitors when Move-Item would have sufficed.

Diagnostic indicator: Persistent hand cramping. Carpal tunnel as a badge of honor. The mouse pad is worn through.

CHAPTER III PERSONALITY DISORDERS
— observation chart 03 —
F60.3
F60.3 · Diagnosis 03

Histrionic GUI Disorder

Dramatic · Suggestible
VERBATIM PATIENT QUOTES
"This looks like hacking."
"I don't want to break anything."
"Is this the dark web?"
"My screen is just text."
# reaction time to a black background:
— gasp recorded at 0.4s
§ Clinical Description

Characterized by dramatic emotional responses to black backgrounds with white text. Patients exclaim phrases such as "this looks like hacking" or "I don't want to break anything."

Often presents alongside an irrational trust in any interface containing rounded corners and pastel gradients.

Tell: Will pay $40/month for a SaaS wrapper around a 12-line shell script.

CHAPTER IV MOOD DISORDERS
— observation chart 04 —
F33.0
F33.0 · Diagnosis 04

Recurring Notepad
Depression

Melancholic · Recurrent
SYSTEM RESOURCE LOG
PS> notepad.exe access.log
WARNING: file size 847 MB
Notepad has stopped responding...
# suggested intervention:
PS> Select-String "ERROR" access.log
→ returned in 0.3s
§ Clinical Description

A melancholic condition wherein the patient repeatedly opens Notepad to "quickly check something" instead of using Get-Content, Select-String, or literally any tool built for the task.

Episodes worsen near log files exceeding 50 MB. The patient may stare at a frozen Notepad window for upwards of nine minutes before contemplating alternatives.

Hallmark: A flat affect that deepens with every blue spinning circle.

CHAPTER V NEURODEVELOPMENTAL
— observation chart 05 —
F84.5
F84.5 · Diagnosis 05

PowerShell Pipeline Apraxia

Cognitive · Treatable
DIAGNOSTIC EXERCISE
# the test pipeline:
PS> Get-Process |
Where-Object CPU -gt 50 |
Stop-Process

# patient response:
"What does the squiggly mean?"
§ Clinical Description

Neurological inability to comprehend that the output of one command can become the input of another. Patients copy-paste between windows manually, sometimes for hours.

The pipe symbol (|) registers as decorative rather than functional. Some patients have been observed retyping output into a fresh command line.

Treatment: Supervised exposure to Get-Process | Where-Object CPU -gt 50 | Stop-Process. Recovery is dramatic when it arrives.

CHAPTER VI ADJUSTMENT DISORDERS
— observation chart 06 —
F43.2
F43.2 · Diagnosis 06

Adjustment Disorder
with Bash Bias

Acquired · Often Academic
TRIGGER RESPONSE LOG
PS> ls
Mode LastWriteTime Name
---- ------------- ----
# patient observed reaction:
*audible sigh*
"That's not real ls."
§ Clinical Description

Acquired in undergraduate Linux courses. Patient becomes physically uncomfortable when ls doesn't behave exactly as expected — despite ls being a perfectly functional alias in pwsh.

Symptoms frequently accompanied by performative sighing, unsolicited mentions of grep, and the phrase "in a real shell..."

Note: Often refuses to acknowledge that pwsh runs natively on Linux and macOS. Cognitive dissonance is severe.

CHAPTER VII ATTENTION DISORDERS
— observation chart 07 —
F90.1
F90.1 · Diagnosis 07

Verb-Noun
Attention Deficit

Cognitive · Retention Issue
SEARCH HISTORY — 30 DAYS
how to list files powershell x 14
powershell list directory contents x 9
pwsh ls equivalent x 12
# the answer, every time:
PS> Get-ChildItem
§ Clinical Description

Inability to retain that PowerShell cmdlets follow a predictable Verb-Noun structure. Patient repeatedly Googles "how to list files powershell" despite having used Get-ChildItem 400 times.

Verbs are forgotten. Nouns are forgotten. The hyphen, somehow, also forgotten.

Prognosis: Improves significantly with tab completion therapy. Aliases (gci, dir, ls) provide symptomatic relief.

CHAPTER VIII SOMATOFORM DISORDERS
— observation chart 08 —
F45.8
F45.8 · Diagnosis 08

Somatic SSH
Aversion

Severe · Often Fatal (to Deadlines)
PATIENT AVOIDANCE PATTERN
# suggested action:
"Just SSH into the box."

# patient instead:
1. Files JIRA ticket
2. Schedules 30m meeting
3. Requests screenshare
ETA: 3 business days
§ Clinical Description

Physical symptoms — sweating, shallow breathing, mild nausea — triggered by the phrase "just SSH into the box."

Patients prefer to file a ticket, schedule a meeting, and request a Teams screenshare instead. The detour can add 72 hours to a 30-second task.

Severity: Frequently fatal to deadlines. Has been known to single-handedly extend project timelines by entire quarters.
